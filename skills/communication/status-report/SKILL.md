# Status Report

## Purpose

Status reports keep sponsors informed and surface blockers before they
become crises. A good report takes 2 minutes to read and answers: "Are we
on track? What do you need from me?"

## Procedure

### 1. Choose the right cadence and audience

- **Weekly** — for active project teams and engaged sponsors
- **Monthly** — for steering committees and peripheral stakeholders
- Calibrate detail level to audience seniority (executives want headlines, PMs want detail)

### 2. Structure the report

Use a consistent structure every time:

#### RAG Status (Red/Amber/Green)
One-line overall assessment:
- **Green** — On track, no intervention needed
- **Amber** — At risk, mitigation underway, may need help
- **Red** — Off track, intervention required

#### Progress (What happened)
- Key accomplishments this period
- Milestones reached
- Decisions made
- Metrics movement

#### Plan (What's next)
- Key activities for next period
- Upcoming milestones and dates
- Planned decisions

#### Blockers and Risks
- Current blockers with owners and resolution dates
- New risks identified
- Risk mitigations in progress

#### Asks (What you need)
- Decisions needed from the reader
- Resources or access required
- Escalations

### 3. Calibrate tone to audience

| Audience | Tone | Detail | Length |
|----------|------|--------|--------|
| Sponsor/Exec | Confident, concise | Headlines only | 1/2 page |
| Steering Committee | Balanced, factual | Key details | 1 page |
| Project Team | Direct, technical | Full detail | 1-2 pages |

### 4. Write clearly

- Lead with the most important information
- Use bullet points, not paragraphs
- Quantify where possible ("3 of 5 stories done" not "good progress")
- Be honest about problems — sponsors hate surprises more than bad news

### 5. Deliver consistently

- Same day, same time, every period
- Same format (recipients learn where to look)
- Flag "no update" periods rather than skipping

## Pitfalls

1. **Burying bad news** — Put blockers and asks near the top. Don't hide problems in paragraph 4 of 5.
2. **Vague progress** — "Working on the system" is not progress. Name specific deliverables and their completion state.
3. **Missing the ask** — Every blocker should have an ask. If you don't need anything, why are you reporting the blocker?
4. **Inconsistent cadence** — Missing a report erodes trust more than reporting "no significant progress."

## Verification

- [ ] RAG status is justified (not always green)
- [ ] Progress items are specific and quantified
- [ ] Every blocker has an owner and target resolution date
- [ ] Asks are clear, actionable, and addressed to specific people
- [ ] Report fits the audience's expected length and detail level
